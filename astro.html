Executive Summary

This report maps Astro Lighting's best-selling products across the UK e-commerce channel, identifying what sells, why, and at what margins — essential intelligence for competitive positioning and range planning.

Key findings: recessed downlights dominate volume (fire-rated, IP65, GU10 format, matt white finish). In decorative categories, iconic products like Mashiko and Parma command premium pricing with substantially tighter retail margins. A clear two-tier margin structure emerges: commodity downlights discounted 50-56% off RRP versus design-led products holding at 16-43% discount.

Market Dynamics & Commercial Insights

8 key findings

GU10 Lamp Format Dominates

80%+ of Astro's best sellers use replaceable GU10 — end users demand bulb choice, colour temperature selection, and smart home compatibility.

→ Product range should lead with GU10 compatibility.

Matt White = 80%+ of Astro's Volume

Almost every confirmed Astro best seller is Matt White. Black is growing as the secondary finish. Chrome/Bronze relevant only in decorative categories.

→ Finish strategy: white-led with black secondary.

Fire Rated + IP65 = Regulatory Sweet Spot

Astro's #1 product outsells all others because it satisfies both fire safety and moisture protection in a single fitting. Building regulations increasingly mandate fire-rated fittings.

→ Dual-rated products maximise addressable market.

Round Outsells Square ~3:1

Every confirmed Astro "best seller" tag goes to round variants. Square consistently ranks secondary.

→ Round format primary, square as extension.

Trade Bulk Purchasing Drives Volume

Astro retailers show "Over 100 in stock" on core SKUs. Individual contractor orders of 10-30+ are standard.

→ Stock depth and trade pricing essential.

Astro Owns Category-Defining Anchors

Mashiko IS bathrooms. Enna IS reading lights. Parma IS plaster walls. These Astro products define their categories — creating both a moat and a clear target spec.

→ Matching Astro's anchor specs is prerequisite for entry.

Specification Enables Margin Protection

Astro products sold through architect/designer specification hold dramatically tighter pricing (Enna at 16% off vs downlights at 56%).

→ Investment in specification channels protects margin.

Accessory Upsell Is Proven

Astro's Tacoma range (interchangeable shades) and GU10-based products (separate bulb purchase) demonstrate the UK market accepts add-on revenue models.

→ Product architecture should include accessory streams.
